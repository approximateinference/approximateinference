In recent years, there have been numerous advances in approximate inference methods, which have enabled Bayesian inference in increasingly challenging scenarios involving complex probabilistic models and large datasets. The 6th Symposium on Advances in Approximate Bayesian Inference (AABI) will discuss this impact of Bayesian inference, connecting both variational and Monte Carlo methods with other fields. We encourage submissions that relate Bayesian inference to the fields of deep learning, reinforcement learning, causal inference, decision processes, Bayesian compression, or differential privacy, among others. We also encourage submissions that contribute to connecting different approximate inference methods.

For the call for papers and submission instructions, click here. This year we will run three submission tracks:

  1. Archival Track – up to 12 pages. Dual submissions are not allowed. Accepted papers to be published in a PMLR proceedings. Authors must attend in person.
  2. Extended Abstract Track – up to 6 pages. Submissions can be under review or published at other venues such as ICLR 2024, ICML 2024, AISTATS 2024, UAI 2024, TMLR and JMLR. However, previously published works must be extended to be eligible. Non-archival. Authors are not required to attend in person.
  3. Poster Fast Track. Papers that have been accepted to any major venue since AABI 2023 (including ICLR 2024, ICML 2024, AISTATS 2024, UAI 2024, TMLR and JMLR) are eligible to be presented as posters at AABI 2024. Authors are required to present in person. This is a non-archival track to avoid conflicts with other venues' dual submission policies. These papers will not appear on the AABI website.
