We invite researchers in machine learning and statistics to participate in the 6th Symposium on Advances in Approximate Bayesian Inference.

Key Dates

  • Submission deadline (archival and extended abstract tracks): 29 March AOE
  • Reviewer assignment: 5 April
  • Review deadline: 26 April
  • Submission deadline (poster fast track): 3 May AOE
  • Author response period (archival track only): 3 May - 10 May
  • Acceptance notification: 24 May
  • Camera ready for accepted submissions: TBD!

Submission Details

We invite researchers to submit their recent work on the development, analysis, or application of approximate Bayesian inference. This year we will run three submission tracks:

  • Archival Track, for new results in papers of up to 12 pages,
  • Extended Abstract Track, for new or extended results in papers of 3-6 pages, and
  • Poster Fast Track, for recently published papers.

Archival Track

The review process will be double-blind – author names need to be anonymized. All accepted archival submissions will be presented in-person as posters and will appear in a PMLR volume. Papers will be made available on the symposium website. Authors will also have the option of recording video summaries, which will also appear on this website.

Dual submission policy: For the archival track, we will not accept papers that are substantially similar to papers that have been previously published, accepted for publication, or submitted in parallel to other conferences or journals.

To submit papers, please use the OpenReview submission link (coming soon). The submission must be up to 12 pages in PDF format using the AABI PMLR two-column style template. Submissions may include a supplement/appendix, but reviewers are not required to read any supplementary material. References may extend as far as needed beyond the page limit. For questions and troubleshooting of the LaTeX template, visit CTAN.

Extended Abstract Track

The review process will be double-blind – author names need to be anonymized. Accepted extended abstract submissions will be given the option to present in person and will not have proceedings to avoid conflict with other venues' double submission policies. Papers will be made available on the symposium website. Authors will also have the option of recording video summaries, which will also appear on this website.

Dual submission policy: If a paper has previously appeared in a journal, workshop, or conference (including the ICML 2024 conference), it should be extended in order to be accepted at AABI 2024. If a paper is currently under review (e.g., in NeurIPS 2024), it can still be submitted to AABI.

To submit papers, please use the OpenReview submission link (coming soon). The submission must be 3-6 pages in PDF format using the AABI PMLR two-column style template. Submissions may include a supplement/appendix, but reviewers are not required to read any supplementary material. References may extend as far as needed beyond the upper page limit. For questions and troubleshooting of the LaTeX template, visit CTAN.

Poster Fast Track

Author names do not need to be anonymized. Papers must have been accepted to a major venue (e.g., NeurIPS, ICML, AISTATS, ICLR, UAI, JMLR, TMLR, etc.) since AABI 2023. Papers must be presented in person and will not have proceedings to avoid conflict with other venues' double submission policies. Papers will be not made available on the symposium website.

To submit papers, please use the submission link (coming soon). The submission must be a PDF formatted as per the camera ready requirements of the original venue.

Questions? Contact us at symposium.approximateinference@gmail.com.
